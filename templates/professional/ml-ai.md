## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Model cards**: Use `<details>` tags to add model cards with metrics (accuracy, F1, params) directly in your README. Recruiters and researchers want to see evaluation results, not just names.

2. **W&B integration**: Replace the generic W&B badge with a live training run link: `<a href="https://wandb.ai/yourusername/project"><img src="https://img.shields.io/badge/View_Training-FFCC00?style=flat-square&logo=weightsandbiases" /></a>`. Shows you track experiments properly.

3. **Paper citations**: If you have publications, use `<img src="https://img.shields.io/badge/Cited_by-X-B31B1B?style=flat-square&logo=arxiv" />` with Google Scholar citation count — academic credibility matters in ML roles.

4. **Hardware setup**: Add a line: `🖥️ Training on: 4x A100 80GB` — shows you have practical infrastructure experience, which is surprisingly rare.

5. **Benchmark leaderboards**: If your model ranks on a leaderboard (GLUE, SuperGLUE, etc.), add: `<img src="https://img.shields.io/badge/GLUE-#2-FF6F00?style=flat-square" />`. Public benchmark rankings = instant credibility.

6. **Conference talks**: Add a row for NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR talks with calendar badges: `<img src="https://img.shields.io/badge/NeurIPS_2024-FF6F00?style=flat-square" />`. Conference presence signals research depth.
