## 🔧 Complete Customization Guide

### Step 1: Basic Information
| What to Replace | Where It Appears | Replace With | Example |
|----------------|-----------------|--------------|---------|
| `<your-name>` | Main heading | Your full name | Priya Sharma |
| `yourusername` | ALL URLs, stats, repo links, visitor counter | Your GitHub username | priyasharma |
| `yourname` | App Store & Google Play developer links | Your developer account name | Priya Sharma |

### Step 2: Social & Store Links
Replace these badge URLs:
- `https://linkedin.com/in/yourusername` → `https://linkedin.com/in/YOUR_LINKEDIN`
- `https://apps.apple.com/developer/yourname` → Your Apple Developer page URL
- `https://play.google.com/store/apps/developer?id=yourname` → Your Google Play Developer page URL

To find your App Store URL: go to App Store Connect → My Apps → any app → App Store page → copy the developer link.

### Step 3: Platform Badges
The row of badges at the top shows your platform expertise:
- Add `Xamarin`, `Cordova`, `Ionic`, `PhoneGap` if you use them
- Add `Material Design`, `SwiftUI`, `Jetpack Compose` badges
- Badge format: `![Label](https://img.shields.io/badge/Label-HEXCODE?style=for-the-badge&logo=logoname&logoColor=white)`

### Step 4: Mobile Stack Table
Customize the platform comparison table:
- Update languages per platform (e.g., add `C#` for Xamarin)
- Add new rows: `⌚ Watch` (WatchKit, Wear OS), `📺 TV` (tvOS, Android TV)
- Remove rows for platforms you don't develop for

### Step 5: Stats
Replace `yourusername` in both stats URLs:
- `https://github-readme-stats.vercel.app/api?username=yourusername&...`
- `https://github-readme-stats.vercel.app/api/top-langs/?username=yourusername&...`

### Step 6: Featured Apps
Update each app card in the table:
| Placeholder | Replace With | Example |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| `📸 SnapApp` | Your app name | 📸 PhotoMaster |
| `Photo editing app` | Short description | Professional photo editor |
| `⭐ 4.8 • 10k+ downloads` | Your ratings | ⭐ 4.7 • 50k+ downloads |
| `https://github.com/yourusername/snapapp` | Your repo URL | `https://github.com/priyasharma/photomaster` |

To add more apps: duplicate the `<td align="center" width="200">` block. Keep 3 per row for best layout.

### Step 7: Available On Badges
The App Store and Google Play badges at the bottom link to your app pages. To link them:
- Wrap each badge in `<a href="YOUR_APP_URL"></a>` to make it clickable
- Example: `<a href="https://apps.apple.com/app/id123456"><img src="..." /></a>`

## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Light theme**: Uses `theme=light` for a clean, mobile-app feel. Change to `theme=default` for slight blue accent, or `theme=tokyonight` for dark mode.
2. **Store badges in footer**: If you have individual apps, replace the generic store badges with app-specific links. Every download counts!
3. **App store links**: To get your exact App Store URL, find your app in the App Store and copy the URL. For Google Play, it's `https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=YOUR.PACKAGE.NAME`.
4. **Mobile stack table**: This table shows you understand multiple platforms. If you specialize (iOS-only or Android-only), remove the other rows.
5. **Add screenshots**: Consider adding GIFs or screenshots of your apps in action. Use `![alt](image_url)` between app cards to show real UI.

## 🚀 One-Click Deploy

1. Copy everything inside the **"Ready-to-Use Code"** block above
2. Go to your profile repo: `github.com/YOUR_USERNAME/YOUR_USERNAME`
3. Open `README.md` and paste over everything
4. Press **Ctrl+H** (or **Cmd+H** on Mac) → find `yourusername` → **Replace All** with your actual GitHub username
5. Replace `<your-name>` and `yourname` (App Store/Play Store developer name)
6. Update platform badges, mobile stack table, and featured apps
7. Commit — your new profile is LIVE! 🎉
