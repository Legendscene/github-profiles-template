### Step 2: Social & Store Links
Replace these badge URLs:
- `https://linkedin.com/in/yourusername` ‚Üí `https://linkedin.com/in/YOUR_LINKEDIN`
- `https://apps.apple.com/developer/yourname` ‚Üí Your Apple Developer page URL
- `https://play.google.com/store/apps/developer?id=yourname` ‚Üí Your Google Play Developer page URL

To find your App Store URL: go to App Store Connect ‚Üí My Apps ‚Üí any app ‚Üí App Store page ‚Üí copy the developer link.

The row of badges at the top shows your platform expertise:
- Add `Xamarin`, `Cordova`, `Ionic`, `PhoneGap` if you use them
- Add `Material Design`, `SwiftUI`, `Jetpack Compose` badges
- Badge format: `![Label](https://img.shields.io/badge/Label-HEXCODE?style=for-the-badge&logo=logoname&logoColor=white)`

- Remove rows for platforms you don't develop for

### Step 5: Stats

## üí° Pro Tips

1. **Light theme**: Uses `theme=light` for a clean, mobile-app feel. Change to `theme=default` for slight blue accent, or `theme=tokyonight` for dark mode.
2. **Store badges in footer**: If you have individual apps, replace the generic store badges with app-specific links. Every download counts!
3. **App store links**: To get your exact App Store URL, find your app in the App Store and copy the URL. For Google Play, it's `https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=YOUR.PACKAGE.NAME`.
4. **Mobile stack table**: This table shows you understand multiple platforms. If you specialize (iOS-only or Android-only), remove the other rows.
5. **Add screenshots**: Consider adding GIFs or screenshots of your apps in action. Use `![alt](image_url)` between app cards to show real UI.
