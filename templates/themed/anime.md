## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Change the Japanese phrase** — The header says "I love writing code". Replace it with your own Japanese phrase using Google Translate or DeepL. Common alternatives: 「よろしく」 (Nice to meet you), 「頑張ります」 (I'll do my best).
2. **Use a real anime quote** — Pick a quote from your favorite anime. Keep it short (1-2 lines) and add the character name + series in the tag. It makes the profile feel personal.
3. **Match badge colors to your waifu** — Use Google Images to find your waifu's color palette, then update shield.io `&color=` values to match. Kurisu = red/chestnut, Asuna = orange/white, etc.
4. **Add an anime theme song** — Embed a YouTube video of your favorite anime OP/ED using `[![...](https://img.youtube.com/vi/ID/0.jpg)](https://youtu.be/ID)` for a clickable thumbnail.
5. **Sync with MyAnimeList** — Keep your MAL profile updated so the link actually shows your list. Bonus: add a "Currently Watching" widget from MAL's API if you're feeling advanced.
6. **Swap synthwave for tokyo-night** — If synthwave is too bright, use `theme=tokyonight` in stats for a more muted, cyberpunk Tokyo vibe that still fits anime.
7. **Anime profile picture** — Use a clear, recognizable anime character as your GitHub avatar (or your face with an anime filter). Consistency across platforms looks polished.
