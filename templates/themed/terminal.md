## ūüí° Pro Tips

1. **Use a monospace font** ‚ÄĒ The ASCII art and tables look best with a monospace font in your README. GitHub renders code blocks in monospace by default, so you're good.
2. **Swap the ASCII logo** ‚ÄĒ Generate your own name ASCII at [patorjk.com](https://patorjk.com/software/taag/) using the "Bloody" or "ANSI Shadow" font to match this theme.
3. **Make it interactive** ‚ÄĒ Add real terminal animations with a GIF capture of your actual terminal workflow for the header instead of static ASCII.
4. **Color your badges** ‚ÄĒ Use `&color=00ff00` in shield.io URLs to tint badges to match the green theme. All social badges should use green tones.
5. **Add a fetch command** ‚ÄĒ Include a `neofetch` or `fastfetch` style ASCII output of your actual system info for extra authenticity.
6. **Hide private email** ‚ÄĒ Use `mailto:` with a disposable or masked email to avoid spam. ProtonMail or SimpleLogin work great.
7. **Keep the joke sections** ‚ÄĒ Visitors love the `$ fortune`, `$ uptime`, and fake `df -h` outputs. It shows personality.
