## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Use `theme=monokai` for stats** — Monokai gives a warm brown/orange palette that matches the coffee aesthetic perfectly. Do not use `chartreuse-dark` (too green) or `synthwave` (too pink for coffee).
2. **The `flat-square` badge style is intentional** — Coffee theme uses `flat-square` (not `for-the-badge`) for social links to keep things cozy and less aggressive. The Brew Stack uses `for-the-badge` to stand out.
3. **Create a real coffee log** — The coffee log is a fun conversation starter. Use actual times and drinks you consume. You can update it weekly and it shows visitors your "coffee personality."
4. **Link your Buy Me A Coffee account** — The whole point of the support section is to get actual donations. Make sure you've created an account at buymeacoffee.com before adding the link. Same for Ko-fi.
5. **Add a caffeine tracker GIF** — Embed a GIF of a coffee cup filling up or a caffeine molecule. Search Giphy for "coffee" or "caffeine". Keep it small with `width="200"` so it's a cute accent.
6. **Warm brown color palette** — The capsule header uses `3c1f10` (dark roast) to `6f4e37` (medium roast). If you customize, stick to the brown spectrum. Accent with `d4a574` (light cream) for text. Avoid blue or green tones.
7. **Change the YAML status daily** — The Daily Grind YAML is meant to be updated. Change `coffee_level` and `status` every morning for a "living" profile that feels active.

10. **Update** the Brew Stack to your actual tech
11. **Commit** — `git commit -am "Add coffee profile"`
12. **Push** — `git push`
13. **Done!** ☕ Your coffee profile is brewing
